How we work together

Three models. One question: what fits your project?

People More works across three engagement structures. Each has a different risk profile, billing pattern, and best-fit scenario. Most clients move between models as their needs evolve. If you are not sure which to choose, the selector at the bottom of this page will help.

Engagement model details

Flexible

Time & Materials

You pay for the hours worked. Scope evolves as the project does. Best for exploratory work, long-running products, or projects where requirements will change.

When it fits
Ongoing product development, post-MVP iteration, research-heavy projects
How billing works
Monthly invoice based on tracked hours at agreed day rates
Delivery cadence
Fortnightly or monthly sprints with backlog review
Risk profile
Budget varies with scope; cost predictability requires good backlog management
Minimum term
No fixed minimum; typically three months for productive engagement
Defined

Fixed Price

A defined scope, a fixed cost, and a committed delivery date. Best for projects with clear specifications, regulatory deadlines, or budget constraints that cannot flex.

When it fits
New product builds with a complete spec, compliance-driven projects, MVP launches with a launch date
How billing works
Milestone-based: agreed amounts paid on delivery of defined project milestones
Delivery cadence
Milestone plan agreed at kick-off; progress reviewed at each milestone gate
Risk profile
Budget and timeline are fixed; scope changes handled through a formal change order process
Minimum term
Project-defined; typically four to twenty weeks depending on scope
Long-term

Dedicated Team

A standing team committed to your product, operating as a permanent extension of your organization. Best for complex, multi-year products where continuity and domain knowledge matter.

When it fits
Managed applications, long-running products, clients replacing or augmenting in-house engineering
How billing works
Fixed monthly retainer covering the agreed team composition
Delivery cadence
Sprint-based or continuous flow; aligned to your existing process
Risk profile
Lowest per-unit cost at scale; highest value when team size is three or more engineers
Minimum term
Six months recommended for team continuity and knowledge transfer

Help me choose

Which model is right for you?

Do you have a complete specification and a fixed budget?

Fixed Price gives you the certainty you need. Scope is locked, cost is locked, and delivery dates are committed.

Is your project likely to evolve, or do you not yet know the full scope?

Time and Materials lets scope shift without contract friction. You pay for what gets built, adjusted as you learn.

Do you need a team long-term, and do you want them to know your product deeply?

Dedicated Team is the most cost-effective option at scale. Engineers who have been on your product for six months outperform a revolving door of contractors.

Still not sure?

Most People More clients start with a short Time and Materials discovery phase to define scope, then move to the model that fits the build. Tell us what you are working on and we will recommend a structure.
