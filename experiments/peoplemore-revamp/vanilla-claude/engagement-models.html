One Partner, Three Ways to Engage

One Partner, Three Ways to Engage

At People More, we do not believe in forcing every client into a single commercial template. A startup validating an MVP has different needs from an enterprise running a multi-year transformation programme — and both deserve a model that serves them fairly.

We have structured our three core engagement models to cover the most common project scenarios. Each has clear trade-offs: we explain those honestly so you can make an informed decision, not just the one that maximises our contract value.

If you are genuinely unsure which model applies to your situation, use the selection guide below — or simply start a conversation with us. We have had that discussion many times and will give you a straight answer.

Engagement Models

Choose Your Model

⏳
Time & Material (T&M)

Flexible, iterative, agile-first

Month-to-month from 2–3 months

You pay for the actual hours worked by our developers and specialists — no more, no less. The scope can evolve as the project progresses, making this model ideal for agile development where requirements are expected to change or emerge over time.

Advantages

  • Maximum flexibility — requirements can change at any sprint boundary
  • Fast kick-off — typically starts within 1–2 weeks
  • Transparent cost visibility with monthly invoices
  • Iterative value delivery — working software shipped regularly

Considerations

  • Less cost predictability — budget planning requires a range
  • Requires active client involvement in sprint reviews
  • Scope creep is a real risk without disciplined backlog management
Best for

Agile product development, MVPs and exploratory prototypes, long-term development partnerships with regular feature releases, projects where the right solution will emerge through iteration.

We use transparent time-tracking tools (typically integrated with your project management platform — Jira, Linear, or equivalent) and provide monthly time reports. You see exactly which tasks were worked on, by whom, for how long. Nothing is black-boxed.
🎯
Fixed Price

Defined scope, predictable cost

4 weeks to 12 months

We agree on a defined scope, budget, and delivery timeline before any development begins. You know exactly what you will get and exactly what it will cost. This model works when requirements are stable, well-documented, and unlikely to change significantly during the build.

Advantages

  • Full cost predictability — the agreed price does not change unless scope changes
  • Clear deliverables and acceptance criteria agreed upfront
  • Minimal client time commitment after scoping phase
  • Strong accountability on our side for rework

Considerations

  • Requires thorough upfront scoping — ambiguous requirements lead to disputes
  • Change requests trigger formal CR process and additional cost
  • Less suited to innovation or exploratory work
  • Risk of over-specifying to feel covered
Best for

Well-defined projects with a clear, stable specification. Integrations and migrations with known inputs and outputs. Fixed-budget initiatives with board approval. Projects with a hard external deadline.

We handle changes through a formal change request (CR) process. We assess the impact of the requested change on timeline and budget, provide you with a written CR document showing the cost and schedule impact, and require your sign-off before any work on the change begins. We aim to keep this process as lightweight and fast as possible.
👥
Dedicated Team / Team Extension

Long-term capacity & control

3-month minimum, typically 12–24+ months

We assemble a hand-picked team of engineers, designers, and/or specialists who work exclusively on your project as a managed unit. In a Team Extension model, individual specialists integrate directly into your existing team. Both give you direct control over the people doing the work, with People More handling HR, administration, and operational continuity.

Advantages

  • Full operational control — you manage priorities, we manage the people
  • Team stability — engineers build deep context about your product and codebase
  • Direct communication — your engineers in your channels and stand-ups
  • Scalable up and down as project phases change

Considerations

  • Requires management capacity on your side — a product owner or CTO who can give direction
  • Team formation takes 2–4 weeks to assemble and onboard
  • Longer minimum commitment — value compounds over time
  • Cultural onboarding investment required, especially in the first month
Best for

Scale-ups growing engineering teams faster than hiring allows. Enterprises with an ongoing product roadmap. Companies lacking a specific specialisation. Long-term product partnerships where continuity matters.

Team continuity is our responsibility, not yours. If a team member leaves People More, we manage the transition and replacement — including handover documentation — as part of our service. If a team member is underperforming relative to the agreed standard, we address it directly and, if necessary, replace them. You will not be left holding an underperforming contract.

Decision Guide

Not Sure Which Model Is Right?
Answer These 5 Questions.

1 Is your project scope fully defined and unlikely to change?
YES Go to Question 2.
NO Time & Material or Dedicated Team are your most natural fits. Continue to Question 3.
2 Is your total budget fixed and formally approved?
YES Fixed Price is likely your best model. Proceed to confirm with us in a scoping call.
NO Go to Question 3.
3 Do you need engineering capacity for more than 6 months?
YES Dedicated Team / Team Extension offers the best long-term value and team stability.
NO Go to Question 4.
4 Do you want direct day-to-day control over the engineers' priorities?
YES Dedicated Team / Team Extension — your priorities, our people.
NO Go to Question 5.
5 Is rapid kick-off more important than cost predictability?
YES Time & Material — we can begin in weeks, not months.
NO Fixed Price — invest time in scoping upfront for cost certainty throughout.
