Model breakdown

Time and Material

Best for agile products where requirements evolve through iteration and learning cycles.

  • Ideal projects: evolving product backlogs and roadmap experimentation.
  • Timeline style: rolling sprints with reprioritization windows.
  • Delivery cadence: weekly coordination, sprint demos, and backlog review.
  • Pricing cue: Not publicly available.

Fixed Price

Best for well-defined initiatives where scope, budget, and acceptance criteria are known upfront.

  • Ideal projects: contained builds, migrations with clear boundaries, compliance-driven work.
  • Timeline style: milestone plan with pre-agreed scope gates.
  • Delivery cadence: checkpoint-based reporting and acceptance reviews.
  • Pricing cue: Not publicly available.

Dedicated Team Extension

Best for organizations that need long-term capacity integrated with internal product ownership.

  • Ideal projects: multi-quarter roadmaps and platform evolution.
  • Timeline style: ongoing engagement aligned to your internal planning cycle.
  • Delivery cadence: direct daily collaboration in client tools and rituals.
  • Pricing cue: Not publicly available.

How to choose quickly

Choose Time and Material if:

  • You expect frequent discovery and scope changes.
  • Speed and adaptability matter more than fixed scope certainty.
  • You want iterative releases with continuous adjustment.

Choose Fixed Price if:

  • You have stable requirements and strict budget governance.
  • The project is well-bounded and acceptance criteria are clear.
  • Procurement requires predefined scope and contractual milestones.

Choose Dedicated Team if:

  • Your roadmap spans multiple quarters.
  • You need domain continuity and direct control over daily priorities.
  • You want to scale engineering without long hiring cycles.
