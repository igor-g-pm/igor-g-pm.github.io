Model 1

Time and Materials (T&M)

Best for agile products where priorities and scope evolve during delivery.

  • Pros: High flexibility, rapid kickoff, transparent effort tracking.
  • Cons: Budget depends on evolving backlog size.
  • Typical contract length: 3-12 months, extendable.
  • Indicative rate band: EUR 450-750 per specialist day.

Model 2

Fixed Price

Best for well-defined scope, timeline, and acceptance criteria.

  • Pros: Predictable budget, clear milestones, easier procurement planning.
  • Cons: Lower flexibility when scope changes significantly.
  • Typical contract length: 2-8 months per project phase.
  • Indicative starting budget: EUR 35k+ for compact product modules.

Model 3

Dedicated Team / Team Extension

Best when you need long-term capacity integrated into your internal product organization.

  • Pros: Full alignment with your roadmap, strong knowledge continuity.
  • Cons: Requires active client-side product leadership and planning.
  • Typical contract length: 6-24 months.
  • Indicative monthly band: EUR 8k-14k per full-time specialist.

Model 4

Outcome-Based Delivery

Best for strategic initiatives where success can be measured through agreed KPIs.

  • Pros: Strong focus on business impact and measurable value.
  • Cons: Needs clear baseline metrics and governance alignment.
  • Typical contract length: 4-12 months with checkpoint gates.
  • Indicative pricing: Base delivery fee + performance-linked component.

Suitability Matrix

Project Context Best Fit Why
Rapidly changing backlog, uncertain feature scope T&M Allows delivery reprioritization without contract resets.
Strict procurement and fixed scope requirements Fixed Price Provides cost certainty and milestone accountability.
Need long-term extension of internal engineering capability Dedicated Team Builds continuity and deeper domain knowledge.
Executive focus on measurable operational or revenue KPIs Outcome-Based Aligns delivery incentives with defined business outcomes.

Pricing bands are directional and should be confirmed during scope definition.

Selection Guide

  1. Scope clarity: If requirements are stable, start with fixed price. If not, consider T&M.
  2. Roadmap horizon: For initiatives over six months, dedicated teams usually reduce ramp-up loss.
  3. Governance style: If executive stakeholders require KPI accountability, evaluate outcome-based delivery.
  4. Budget posture: Choose predictability (fixed) or adaptability (T&M/dedicated) based on risk appetite.
